FLASH FLOOD DETECTION DEVICE

During my final year at Texas A&M our senior design group was tasked with designing a device that could detect flood levels on a road or underpass in real time in order to send that data to drivers to avoid accidents or possible endangerment. Our team came up with a crude mock design for what they wanted the device to look like. As the lead CAD designer of this project, I was able to take this idea and make it into a reality. My team members gave me several components that needed to be used in the design and I was able to model them and take these components along with designing other various components such as a sensor stand in order to create a final assembly model of the design. This 3D model was taken and used as the blueprint for the functioning test model used in our final demonstration.

Advanced metal manufacturing

In my advanced CAD design class, our group was tasked with designing any product that had 6 degrees of freedom, used different manufacturing techniques and also had a low budget and ease of manufacturing. I was the assistant cad designer along with another individual and the budget analyzer for the project. We decided to design a dual monitor stand that had 6 degrees of freedom. Our team took items and hardware from McMaster-Carr in order to simplify he design but also used created parts such as the brackets and stand itself. Manufacturing techniques that were used were pipe bending, welding, and sheet metal folding in order to create this design. The estimated cost for this product was under budget for a total cost around $100. Our team met all the combined criteria in order to successfully design the dual monitor stand that we chose.

Weldment & Stress Analysis

For the weldment and stress analysis project I was tasked with creating a frame assembly using solidworks weldment feature in order to assemble a bench that can handle a load of 4-5 people. Above you can see the final assembled product of the weldment frame and complementing components such as the wood planks and rivets that hold the planks to the frame. A stress and defelction analysis was performed on the bench with a total deflection of 0.159 inches being the result. This result was well within the given parameters of the assignment and the weakest point of the assembly was the wooden planks of the bench while the frame wel withstood the applied force.

vertical Motor safety Stand

I was tasked with modifying an existing safety stand for vertical motor inspection. The existing stand was not very versatile, it did not accommodate for the variation in different sized vertical motors to be inspected. The existing design for this stand also created a trip hazard and could potentially put someoneâ€™s life in danger in case a motor were to actually fall. I was tasked with modifying the existing design by adding a rail system and reinforcing an I-beam so the motor could fall on the rail system in the event one of the straps these motors are usually hung from would snap. This safety stand is meant to be used as a secondary safety precaution and was not designed to hold the motor. The rail system is adjustable for different sized motors and features a brake system in order to stop the rails from moving in the event of an accident.

Sheet Metal Locker Design

For this project I was tasked with designing a locker set for workers that can house their phone/personal items, a jacket/work shirt and a set of boots that they will be using. The design criteria for this project were to use the Sheetmetal features Solidworks has to offer and to fit all the items listed above. The body of the locker and door were made using the Sheetmetal feature in Solidworks. The rest of the parts were created using the normal part creation in Solidworks and the mounting hardware was used from McMaster-Carr. The locker was designed to be wall mounted with a hanger on the back. This locker features a shelf to store personal items in and also has a hook for clothing to be hung.
